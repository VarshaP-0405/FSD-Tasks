Basilisk - The King of Serpents

A Deadly Gaze: The Basilisk is a legendary serpent-like creature known for its lethal stare, which can kill or petrify those who meet its eyes. It is often described as the "King of Serpents."

Appearance: Traditionally, the Basilisk is depicted as a large serpent or a reptilian creature with the crown-like crest on its head. Some myths describe it as having the body of a serpent and the head of a rooster.

Symbolism: The Basilisk represents death, danger, and supreme power over lesser creatures. It was believed to be one of the most feared mythical beasts.

Weakness: According to legends, the Basilisk could be defeated by a weasel or by seeing its own reflection in a mirror, which would turn its deadly gaze back upon itself.

Parentage: Some myths claim that a Basilisk is born from an egg laid by a rooster and incubated by a serpent or a toad.

Other Names: Often referred to as "The Serpent King," the Basilisk remains one of the most infamous mythical creatures.
