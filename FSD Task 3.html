Welcome, learn more about Paris

Paris (French pronunciation: [paʁi] ⓘ) is the capital and largest city of France. With an estimated population of 2,048,472 residents in January 2025[3] in an area of more than 105 km2 (41 sq mi),[4] Paris is the fourth-most populous city in the European Union, the ninth-most populous city in Europe and the 30th most densely populated city in the world in 2022.[5] Since the 17th century, Paris has been one of the world's major centres of finance, diplomacy, commerce, culture, fashion, and gastronomy. Because of its leading role in the arts and sciences and its early adaptation of extensive street lighting, it became known as the City of Light in the 19th century.

Urbanism and architecture

Paris is one of the few world capitals that has rarely seen destruction by catastrophe or war. As a result, even its earliest history is visible in its streetmap, and centuries of rulers adding their respective architectural marks on the capital has resulted in an accumulated wealth of history-rich monuments and buildings whose beauty plays a large part in giving Paris the reputation it has today.[133] At its origin, before the Middle Ages, Paris was composed of several islands and sandbanks in a bend of the Seine. Of those, two remain today: Île Saint-Louis and the Île de la Cité. A third one is the 1827 artificially created Île aux Cygnes.



🌍 Popular Places To Visit 🌍

  • Eiffel Tower (Tour Eiffel): The most famous symbol of Paris, offering panoramic city views.

  • Louvre Museum (Musée du Louvre): The world's most visited art museum, housing masterpieces like the Mona Lisa.

  • Notre Dame Cathedral: A stunning example of Gothic architecture, though currently undergoing restoration after a fire.

  • Arc de Triomphe: A triumphal arch commemorating those who fought for France in the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars.

  • Palace of Versailles: The opulent former royal residence of French monarchs, located just outside Paris.

  • Montmartre: A charming, artistic neighborhood known for its Sacré-Coeur Basilica and lively atmosphere.

  • Latin Quarter (Quartier Latin): A historic student district with a vibrant atmosphere and the Sorbonne University.

🌍 Popular Food Items🌍

  • Croissant-Flaky, buttery, and perfect with coffee.

  • Pain au Chocolat – A croissant-like pastry filled with chocolate.

  • French Onion Soup – Rich beef broth with caramelized onions and melted cheese.

  • Escargots de Bourgogne – Snails cooked with garlic, parsley, and butter.

  • Coq au Vin – Chicken slow-cooked in red wine with mushrooms and bacon.
